## Prediction

While I know nothing of carbon fiber nanotubes (CFNTs), I saw some videos 8-10 years ago of soft-muscle actuators built from CFNTs and was very impressed. I had an idea that I've since seen implemented by Tesla in their autonomous vehicles, where a local system/ANN learns the "body". I could equip basic software to control a robot, but a vast harness of nodes that control electrical delivery and syncronization of muscles/current could be learned by a local ANN -- one that's an expert on *that* shell -- on *that* sub-muscle sequence, and I create a heirarchy of neural nodes/intelligences that coordinate the activation and function of a robotic asset. This is what I need to learn. I don't think the clunk mechanics of the designs of other robots is the right direction. I think it's soft-muscle actuators, CFNTs, and desiging/discovery completely novel modalities (non-human, or even non-terrestrial... let the design evolve to master its environment). I think the limitations of terrestrial organisms are constraints to be broken, not emulated. That design philosphy should inform all asset creation for manual labor, war, etc., but I think a core product that will sell will be realistic human robotics to get started (a product people will understand).
